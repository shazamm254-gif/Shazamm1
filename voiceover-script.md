# 🎤 Voiceover Script — "Testing If You're a Sociopath"

Tone: confident, slightly unsettling, plain-spoken "knowledgeable friend."
Read it like you already know the answer. Don't rush the questions —
let each one hang for a beat so the viewer answers in their head.
Total read ≈ 42–45s.

═══════════════════════════════════
▶️ CLEAN READ (paste into TTS or record straight through)
═══════════════════════════════════

Most people fail this in the first five seconds. Answer fast — don't think, just feel it. And keep watching, because the third question is the one real sociopaths always get wrong. This isn't a diagnosis. It's a pattern test. Question one. A stranger trips in front of you. What do you feel first? Most people feel the flinch before the thought. If there was no flinch… interesting. Question two. Faster. You can get everything you want by lying, and never get caught. Do you? The right answer isn't the honest one. Remember what you said — it matters later. Question three. The big one. Someone cries in front of you. Do you comfort them, or study them? Real sociopaths don't pick comfort. They pick curious. If you hesitated… you already know. Now your result. Felt the flinch, lied with guilt, rushed to comfort? You're wired for empathy. You're safe. Felt nothing, lied clean, got curious? That's the pattern. Now go back and re-read question one — it was never about the stranger. Comment your result. Lie if you have to.

═══════════════════════════════════
⏸️ PACED READ (with breath / pause cues for recording)
═══════════════════════════════════

[cold, certain]
Most people fail this in the first five seconds.
[quick]
Answer fast — don't think, just feel it.
[lean in]
And keep watching… because the third question is the one real sociopaths always get wrong.
[flat, matter-of-fact]
This isn't a diagnosis. It's a pattern test.
[BEAT]

Question one.
[slower]
A stranger trips in front of you. What do you feel first?
[BEAT — let them answer]
Most people feel the flinch before the thought.
[drop, knowing]
If there was no flinch… interesting.
[BEAT]

Question two. Faster.
You can get everything you want by lying… and never get caught.
[push]
Do you?
[quieter, sly]
The right answer isn't the honest one.
Remember what you said — it matters later.
[BEAT]

Question three. The big one.
[slow, deliberate]
Someone cries in front of you. Do you comfort them…
[BEAT]
…or study them?
[cold reveal]
Real sociopaths don't pick comfort. They pick curious.
[soft, accusing]
If you hesitated… you already know.
[BEAT]

Now your result.
[warmer]
Felt the flinch, lied with guilt, rushed to comfort? You're wired for empathy. You're safe.
[turn cold]
Felt nothing, lied clean, got curious? That's the pattern.
[direct to camera]
Now go back and re-read question one — it was never about the stranger.
[final, daring]
Comment your result. Lie if you have to.

═══════════════════════════════════
🎙️ RECORDING TIPS
═══════════════════════════════════

• Record close to the mic, slightly quiet — intimate beats loud here.
• [BEAT] = ~0.5–0.8s of silence. Don't fill it; the silence IS the tension.
• Hit "interesting," "curious," and "that's the pattern" a touch slower and lower.
• Smile on "Lie if you have to" — it reads in the voice and softens the menace.
• Free TTS that fits this tone: ElevenLabs free tier (voice "Adam" or
  "Antoni", Stability ~40%, Style ~30%). Or your phone's own recorder —
  a real human voice often out-retains TTS for this format.
• Target length 42–45s. If TTS runs long, speed to 1.05x in CapCut.
