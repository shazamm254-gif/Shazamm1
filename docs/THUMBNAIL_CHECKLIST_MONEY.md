# Money Decoded — Thumbnail & First-Frame Checklist

For Shorts, the **first frame** *is* the thumbnail in most places — it's what
shows in the feed, on your channel grid, and in search. Treat frame 1 of every
Short as a thumbnail you have to earn the swipe with. (You can also set a custom
thumbnail that appears on the channel grid and search; do both.)

Run this checklist on every upload before it goes out.

---

## The 5-second test

Before posting, glance at your first frame for 5 seconds and ask:
**"Would a stranger scrolling at full speed stop for this?"**
If you hesitate, it's not done. The image must promise the payoff instantly.

---

## Must-haves (every thumbnail / first frame)

- [ ] **One clear subject, large in frame.** A single number, object, or chart — not a busy collage. If a viewer can't tell what they're looking at in half a second, redo it.
- [ ] **The most striking visual, not a title card.** Lead with the credit card statement, the pyramid diagram, the doubling chart — never a logo or "Episode 4" slate.
- [ ] **High contrast.** Subject pops off the background. Clean flat-design with one bright accent color suits this niche.
- [ ] **Reads at thumbnail size.** Shrink it to the size of a fingernail — is the subject still obvious? Detail disappears at feed size; shape and contrast survive.
- [ ] **Curiosity, not explanation.** The image should raise a question ("wait, that's how much?"), not answer it.
- [ ] **A dollar sign, percentage, or big number.** Concrete figures ("300% APR", "10 YEARS", "$400") stop scrolls harder than abstract concepts.

---

## Text on the image (keep it minimal)

- [ ] **3–4 words max**, if any. The feed is small; a sentence is unreadable.
- [ ] **Big, bold, high-contrast font.** One font across all Shorts for brand recognition. White or dark text with a strong stroke/shadow reads on any background.
- [ ] **Top third or a clear corner.** Keep text clear of the bottom — the Shorts UI (title, channel, buttons) covers the lower portion and right edge.
- [ ] **A number or stakes word lands hard.** "300% APR", "10+ YEARS", "$400 TEST", "THE MINIMUM TRAP" — concrete and dramatic beats vague.
- [ ] **Don't repeat the title verbatim.** The title is already shown; the on-image text should add a second hook, not duplicate.

---

## Brand consistency (so your grid looks like a channel, not a pile)

- [ ] **Same font + same text style** on every Short.
- [ ] **A consistent color signature** — e.g. dark background with one accent color (green for "growth," red for "traps"). Your channel grid should be recognizable as *yours* at a glance.
- [ ] **Optional corner badge** for the pillar name (Psychology / How Money Moves / Debt / Investing / Scams) so viewers learn your formats.
- [ ] **No clutter, no watermarks, no stock-art look.** Clean motion-graphic style is your advantage — make it feel like a premium explainer, not a stock finance blog.

---

## Niche-specific wins (money)

- [ ] **Lead with the number, not the concept.** "$45 fee" or "300% APR" in giant text beats "Payday Loan Math" as a subject.
- [ ] **Before/after or comparison framing** — a shrinking paycheck next to a growing bill, two bars showing "index fund vs. actively managed fund." Contrast is your strongest hook.
- [ ] **Familiar objects, not stock photography** — a credit card, a paycheck stub, a calculator — rendered in your consistent graphic style, not generic stock imagery.
- [ ] **A visual "gotcha"** — a pie chart mostly interest, a pyramid mostly empty at the bottom, a line chart with a gap where the "best days" were missed. The visual should make the trap or truth obvious at a glance.
- [ ] **Avoid generic money clichés** — stacks of cash, a rising stock arrow with no context. Make frame 1 a *specific*, dramatic number or mechanism.

---

## Quick reject list (redo the frame if any are true)

- ✗ Subject is small or off-center
- ✗ First frame is a title card, logo, or black intro
- ✗ Text is more than ~4 words or hard to read shrunk down
- ✗ Important text/subject sits in the bottom or far-right (UI covers it)
- ✗ Low contrast — subject blends into background
- ✗ The image answers the question instead of raising one
- ✗ It doesn't look like the rest of your channel

---

### Workflow

1. Pick your single most arresting generated frame as frame 1 (usually the big number or the visual "gotcha").
2. Add ≤4 words of high-contrast text in your channel font, top third.
3. Shrink to fingernail size — still obvious? If not, fix contrast/size.
4. Set a matching custom thumbnail (for grid + search).
5. Confirm the title (run `tools/optimize_metadata.py`) doesn't just repeat the on-image text.
