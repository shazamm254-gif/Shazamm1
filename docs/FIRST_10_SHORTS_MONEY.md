# Money Decoded — Your First 10 Shorts (ready to produce)

Ten complete, ready-to-shoot Shorts across all five pillars in
`tools/niche-money.json`. Each is built to win the first 2 seconds and loop
or provoke a comment at the end.

**How to read each script:**
- **HOOK (0–2s)** — exact words on screen + said aloud in frame 1. 80% of the battle.
- **VO** — full voiceover, timed to ~15–30s. Narrate it direct and a little urgent — like someone finally explaining the thing nobody told you.
- **ON-SCREEN** — big text (most people watch muted first).
- **VISUALS** — what to generate; cut every 1.5–3s.
- **END** — the loop line or question (drives re-watch + comments).
- **TITLE / DESC** — paste-ready. Run through `tools/optimize_metadata.py` before posting.

Narration target: ~2.5 words/second (a 20s Short ≈ 50 words). Keep it tight.
These facts are grounded in real personal-finance mechanics — dramatized,
not fabricated. Round numbers where exact figures vary; don't invent stats.

## Production notes

- **Voice:** direct, calm, a little urgent — like a friend finally explaining the thing nobody told you. Not preachy, not hype.
- **Music:** one low, steady, modern bed under everything — same track across the series for instant brand recognition.
- **Visual style:** clean flat-design animation, high contrast, one clear focal number or object per shot. Keep the palette consistent so your channel grid reads as *yours*.
- **Caption/comment prompt:** end each post's caption with a question to farm comments, e.g. "Which one caught you off guard — the minimum payment trap or the payday loan math?"
- **Batch these.** Generate all 10 sets of visuals in one session, record all 10 VOs back to back, schedule one per day. Never produce one at a time.
- **Validate packaging:** before each upload, run
  `python tools/optimize_metadata.py --title "<title>"` and fix any ✗ it flags.
- **Fact-check before posting.** Rates, APRs, and statistics shift over time and by lender/fund — keep the mechanism accurate and round the numbers rather than citing a figure you can't currently verify.
