# Dream What-Ifs — Thumbnail & First-Frame Checklist

For Shorts, the **first frame** *is* the thumbnail in most places — it's what
shows in the feed, on your channel grid, and in search. Treat frame 1 of every
Short as a thumbnail you have to earn the swipe with. (You can also set a custom
thumbnail that appears on the channel grid and search; do both.)

Run this checklist on every upload before it goes out.

---

## The 5-second test

Before posting, glance at your first frame for 5 seconds and ask:
**"Would a stranger scrolling at full speed stop for this?"**
If you hesitate, it's not done. The image must promise the payoff instantly.

---

## Must-haves (every thumbnail / first frame)

- [ ] **One clear subject, large in frame.** A single figure, brain diagram, or dream image — not a busy collage. If a viewer can't tell what they're looking at in half a second, redo it.
- [ ] **The most striking visual, not a title card.** Lead with the shadow figure, the frozen sleeper, the surreal dreamscape — never a logo or "Episode 4" slate.
- [ ] **High contrast.** Subject pops off the background. Dark, night-toned backgrounds with a softly glowing subject suit this niche well.
- [ ] **Reads at thumbnail size.** Shrink it to the size of a fingernail — is the subject still obvious? Detail disappears at feed size; silhouette and contrast survive.
- [ ] **Curiosity, not explanation.** The image should raise a question ("wait, what is that?"), not answer it.
- [ ] **A hint of the uncanny.** A still figure at the foot of a bed, eyes open while the body doesn't move — familiar bedroom scenes made subtly wrong stop scrolls.

---

## Text on the image (keep it minimal)

- [ ] **3–4 words max**, if any. The feed is small; a sentence is unreadable.
- [ ] **Big, bold, high-contrast font.** One font across all Shorts for brand recognition. White or pale text with a dark stroke/shadow reads on any background.
- [ ] **Top third or a clear corner.** Keep text clear of the bottom — the Shorts UI (title, channel, buttons) covers the lower portion and right edge.
- [ ] **A stakes phrase lands hard.** "CAN'T MOVE", "STILL DREAMING", "6 HOURS OFF", "10 SECONDS ASLEEP" — concrete and personal beats vague.
- [ ] **Don't repeat the title verbatim.** The title is already shown; the on-image text should add a second hook, not duplicate.

---

## Brand consistency (so your grid looks like a channel, not a pile)

- [ ] **Same font + same text style** on every Short.
- [ ] **A consistent color signature** — deep blues/purples/night tones with one soft glowing accent. Your channel grid should be recognizable as *yours* at a glance.
- [ ] **Optional corner badge** for the pillar name (Lucid Dreaming / Sleep Paralysis / Dream Meaning / Sleep Science / Unexplained) so viewers learn your formats.
- [ ] **No clutter, no watermarks, no stock-art look.** Soft, dreamlike AI generation is your advantage — make it feel like a half-remembered dream, not a generic sleep-hygiene infographic.

---

## Niche-specific wins (dreams & sleep)

- [ ] **A familiar bedroom made strange** — a normal room with one wrong detail (a still shadow in the corner, eyes open in the dark) reads instantly and is deeply relatable.
- [ ] **Blur the edge between dream and real** — soft, slightly warped edges on dream imagery signal "this is a dream" without needing text.
- [ ] **Personal framing over abstract science** — a single sleeping figure beats a clinical brain diagram as the lead image; save diagrams for a secondary beat mid-Short.
- [ ] **Deep blues/purples + one soft glow** — moody night tones with a single warm or pale light source. Keep this palette consistent so the grid reads as *yours*.
- [ ] **Avoid generic "sleepy" clichés** — a yawning stock photo or a plain moon-and-stars graphic looks like every wellness account. Make frame 1 a *specific*, unsettling or curious moment.

---

## Quick reject list (redo the frame if any are true)

- ✗ Subject is small or off-center
- ✗ First frame is a title card, logo, or black intro
- ✗ Text is more than ~4 words or hard to read shrunk down
- ✗ Important text/subject sits in the bottom or far-right (UI covers it)
- ✗ Low contrast — subject blends into background
- ✗ The image answers the question instead of raising one
- ✗ It doesn't look like the rest of your channel

---

### Workflow

1. Pick your single most arresting generated frame as frame 1 (usually the "wrong detail" moment).
2. Add ≤4 words of high-contrast text in your channel font, top third.
3. Shrink to fingernail size — still obvious? If not, fix contrast/size.
4. Set a matching custom thumbnail (for grid + search).
5. Confirm the title (run `tools/optimize_metadata.py`) doesn't just repeat the on-image text.
