# Dream What-Ifs — Your First 10 Shorts (ready to produce)

Ten complete, ready-to-shoot Shorts across all five pillars in
`tools/niche-dreams.json`. Each is built to win the first 2 seconds and loop
or provoke a comment at the end.

**How to read each script:**
- **HOOK (0–2s)** — exact words on screen + said aloud in frame 1. 80% of the battle.
- **VO** — full voiceover, timed to ~15–30s. Narrate it curious and a little eerie — someone walking you through a hypothetical that turns out to be real.
- **ON-SCREEN** — big text (most people watch muted first).
- **VISUALS** — what to generate; cut every 1.5–3s.
- **END** — the loop line or question (drives re-watch + comments).
- **TITLE / DESC** — paste-ready. Run through `tools/optimize_metadata.py` before posting.

Narration target: ~2.5 words/second (a 20s Short ≈ 50 words). Keep it tight.
These facts are grounded in real sleep science — dramatized, not fabricated.
Where the science is contested (like dream meaning), say so honestly.

## Production notes

- **Voice:** curious, a little hushed and eerie — like someone telling you a true story that sounds made up. Not clinical, not spooky-for-spooky's-sake.
- **Music:** one soft, ambient, slightly unsettling bed under everything (think: sparse piano/synth pads) — same track across the series for instant brand recognition.
- **Visual style:** deep blues/purples/night tones, soft glow around dream imagery, a slightly surreal or dreamlike blur on transitions. Keep the palette consistent so your channel grid reads as *yours*.
- **Caption/comment prompt:** end each post's caption with a question to farm comments, e.g. "Have you ever had sleep paralysis? Tell me what you saw."
- **Batch these.** Generate all 10 sets of visuals in one session, record all 10 VOs back to back, schedule one per day. Never produce one at a time.
- **Validate packaging:** before each upload, run
  `python tools/optimize_metadata.py --title "<title>"` and fix any ✗ it flags.
- **Be honest about contested science.** Dream-meaning theories are genuinely debated — say so rather than presenting one theory as settled fact.
