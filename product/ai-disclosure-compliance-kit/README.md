# The AI Disclosure & Compliance Kit

**Don't get your channel suspended over a checkbox you didn't know existed.**
Every major platform now *requires* creators to disclose realistic
AI-generated or AI-altered content — YouTube's "Altered or synthetic content"
toggle, TikTok's AIGC label, Meta's AI-content labels — and the rules are
scattered across policy pages, get updated without warning, and are widely
misunderstood. This kit turns "wait, do I need to flag this one?" into a
30-second checklist.

Built for exactly the kind of channel this repo helps you run: **faceless,
AI-generated content**, where every single upload is a candidate for
disclosure.

## Quick start

1. **Read `GUIDE.md` §1–2** — understand *why* disclosure exists and the
   one-question test that decides whether any given video needs it.
2. **Run the flowchart in `TEMPLATES.md` §1** on your last 5 uploads.
   You'll know in under 10 minutes whether you have a compliance gap.
3. **Turn on the disclosure toggle** for any upload that needs it, using the
   platform steps in `GUIDE.md` §3.
4. **Paste the description/pinned-comment templates** (`TEMPLATES.md` §2) into
   your upload workflow so this becomes a checklist item, not a judgment call,
   forever.
5. **Keep the audit log** (`TEMPLATES.md` §4) so if a platform ever asks, you
   have a record of good-faith compliance.

---

## The one thing to remember

**Disclosure is not a penalty and it does not hurt your reach.** Platforms
have said publicly that labeling doesn't reduce distribution — *not*
labeling when required is what gets content removed, demonetized, or your
account suspended. Flag it, don't skip it, move on.

> Policies here move fast. Treat this kit as the framework and the checklist
> — always confirm current specifics on each platform's official policy page
> before you rely on them for a monetization-critical decision.
