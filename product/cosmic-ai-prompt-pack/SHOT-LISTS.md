# Shot Lists — prompts sequenced for your first 10 Shorts

Each block matches a script in `docs/FIRST_10_SHORTS.md`, broken into 4 shots in
order. Generate each shot (image or video), append the master style suffix from
`STYLE-GUIDE.md`, use 9:16, and cut between them every 1.5–3s over the voiceover.

This is the fastest path from script to finished Short: copy, generate, edit.

---

## 1 — Falling Into a Black Hole

1. A slow drift toward a black hole's glowing orange accretion disk, light bending around it *(use as the first frame)*
2. A human silhouette stretching into a thin thread of light falling toward the dark
3. The accretion disk warping the star field into a glowing ring around absolute black
4. Pure black filling the frame, a single faint warm glow at the edge

## 2 — The Largest Thing in the Universe

1. Earth as a small blue dot against the black *(first frame)*
2. Slow zoom out: the solar system, then the spiral of the Milky Way
3. A vast glowing web of galaxies stretching across the entire frame
4. A tiny labeled dot in the web with everything else dwarfing it

## 3 — If the Sun Vanished

1. The Sun blinking out, the frame falling into darkness *(first frame, with "8 MINUTES" text)*
2. A dark Earth drifting off its orbit in a straight line into deep space
3. Frozen oceans, cracked ice stretching to a black horizon
4. A frost-covered dead city under a starlit black sky

## 4 — The Last Star

1. A single dim red dwarf alone in absolute black *(first frame)*
2. The red star dimming further, its glow shrinking
3. The star flickering out, the frame going fully dark
4. A slow pan across pure empty blackness, no stars at all

## 5 — When Black Holes Collide

1. Two black holes spiraling toward each other, accretion disks tilted and glowing *(first frame)*
2. A blinding flash at the moment of merger at the center of warped space
3. Rings of gravitational waves rippling outward through a glowing space-time grid
4. A faint wave washing over a tiny distant blue Earth

## 6 — Planets With No Sun

1. A dark frozen rogue planet drifting through starless black *(first frame)*
2. The rogue planet passing a distant sun, its surface still unlit
3. The rogue planet nearing a small blue Earth
4. Earth nudged off its orbit, drifting into the dark

## 7 — The Monster at the Center

1. Flying down through the glowing spiral arms of the Milky Way *(first frame)*
2. Stars whipping in fast orbits around a dark central point
3. The silhouette of the central black hole, Sagittarius A*, a perfect dark sphere
4. A tiny dot (the Sun) tracing its slow orbit far out from the center

## 8 — The Loneliest Place

1. Pull back from a dense cluster of galaxies into a vast empty black sphere *(first frame)*
2. A single faint galaxy drifting alone in the enormous void
3. Endless dark, almost no light anywhere in the frame
4. The lone galaxy as a tiny smudge, surrounded by total emptiness

## 9 — The Deadliest Event

1. A collapsing star, its core imploding in its final instant *(first frame)*
2. Twin beams of intense light firing out from the star's poles
3. A beam sweeping across space toward a tiny blue Earth
4. Earth's atmosphere flaring and being stripped away by the beam

## 10 — The Great Attractor

1. Thousands of galaxies streaming in one direction across the frame *(first frame)*
2. An unseen mass hidden behind the bright glare of the Milky Way's core
3. A dark region with a question mark, galaxies bending toward it
4. Galaxies still flowing inward toward the unknown point

---

### Workflow recap

1. Copy a shot prompt → append the master style suffix → set 9:16 → generate.
2. Do all 4 shots for one Short, pick the best variation of each.
3. Lay them over the voiceover, cut every 1.5–3s, add your music bed + captions.
4. Run the title through `tools/optimize_metadata.py`, then schedule.

Produce all 10 Shorts' shots in one batch session — don't do them one at a time.
